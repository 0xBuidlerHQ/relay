# Why Relay

`React` makes building event-driven UIs easy, but `coordinating` multi-step `flows—forms`, `payments`, `blockchain transactions` or anything async, quickly turns into tangled `useEffect` chains, manual timers, or duplicated state. 

Relay was created to give those flows an `explicit structure`: each step is a `typed` operation, `Relay` keeps status and payload data in a Zustand-backed `store`, and the UI can drive navigation with helpers instead of ad-hoc callbacks.

## Relay at a glance

- **Typed steps** declare their `success` and `error` payloads.
- **Flows** are ordered step sequences that `Relay` executes one by one.
- **Stores** live per `createRelay` key and expose `selectors` and `helpers`.
- **Status tracking** surfaces operation `states`.

---

Relay is intentionally `un-opinionated` about how you build your steps while still giving you atomic control over each transition.
